PREDICTIONS

The following graphs represent predictions for the spreading of the virus within the West African countries affected. These predictions were calculated using the SIR Model of Disease spreading, which was adapted to use information mined from Twitter.

According to predictions based on the SIR model of disease spreading, Liberia will have an increasing number of weekly cases for the first 11 months after the outbreak. Following this threshold weekly cases will begin decreasing.
According to predictions based on the SIR model of disease spreading, Guinea will have an increasing number of weekly cases for the first 18 months after the outbreak. Following this threshold weekly cases will begin decreasing.
According to predictions based on the SIR model of disease spreading, Sierra Leone will have an increasing number of weekly cases for the first 16 months after the outbreak. Following this threshold weekly cases will begin decreasing.

ZMAPP

ZMapp is an experimental drug developed by Mapp Biopharmaceutical. ZMappTM is composed of three “humanized” monoclonal antibodies manufactured in plants, specifically Nicotiana [tobacco]. It is an optimized cocktail combining the best components of MB-003 (Mapp) and ZMAb (Defyrus/PHAC). It is manufactured at Kentucky BioProcessing in Owensboro. Production of the drug takes several weeks and only produces a small amount. Current side effects of the drug include chest tightness and increased heart rate. More side effects will become known with continuous observations.
